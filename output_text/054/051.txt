Henry confided to Cromwell that he had not consummated the marriage, saying, "I liked her before not well, but now I like her much worse".In February 1540, speaking to the Countess of Rutland, Anne praised the King as a kind husband, saying: "When he comes to bed he kisseth me, and he taketh me by the hand, and biddeth me 'Good night, sweetheart'; and in the morning kisseth me and biddeth 'Farewell, darling.'"
