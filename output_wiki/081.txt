Vivian Alderson Metcalfe (5 March 1906 â€“ 28 December 1967) was an Anglo-Irish cricketer.  Metcalfe was a right-handed batsman.  He was born in Saltburn-by-the-Sea, Yorkshire.
Metcalfe made a single first-class appearance for Wales against the touring West Indians in 1928.  In this match, he scored 18 runs in the Welsh first-innings before being dismissed by James Neblett, while in their second-innings he wasn't required to bat.  He later made three first-class appearances for Ireland in 1936, playing against Scotland, the touring Indians and the Marylebone Cricket Club.  In his three first-class appearances for Ireland, he scored 63 runs at an average of 10.50, with a high score of 36.He died in Addlestone, Surrey on 28 December 1967.
